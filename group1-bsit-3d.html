Bobot:

An Interactive Filipino Sign Language App

Rationale

     The Filipino Sign Language (FSL) has been recognized as the national sign language with the recent passing of the Republic Act no. 11106 or The Filipino Sign Language Act,  which declares The Filipino Sign Language As The National Sign Language Of The Filipino Deaf And The Official Sign Language Of Government In All Transactions Involving The Deaf, And Mandating Its Use In Schools, Broadcast Media, And Workplaces. One of the main concerns is that most Filipinos are unaware that the Philippines has an official sign language, and according to Lintag (2019, as cited in Guno, 2019) there is no specific mention nor implementation plan of FSL in the present K to 12 Department of Education Order.

     The system aims to address the deficiencies in awareness. It works when a user provides an email and password to sign up to the system, which is then stored to the database online. The main feature of the system is the quiz functionality which is tier-based and has progress lock, which means that the user cannot proceed unless they finish the current level that they are taking. All the userâ€™s progress is saved and attached to their account, which they can also access on other devices when they login using their account. The FSL gestures are represented by images in the .gif and .png format which will then be stored in the database online. Information is retrieved when a quiz item is generated to the users when they are currently inside a question.

Objectives

This study aimed to create a cross-platform Filipino Sign Language Quiz App with React-Native.

Specifically, this study is aimed to:

   Allow users to Sign-up using their email and given password.

   Enable users to Log-in, and are validated.

   Translate user-inputted words into .gif format.

  Enable the system to render all the topics from the database.

  Display the  progress of the user.

  Store user progress when they finish a topic.

  Allow users to view lectures from certain topics.

  Allow users to take quizzes from certain topics.
